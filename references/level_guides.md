# Level Guides

## English CEFR

| Level | Vocabulary | Sentence Shape | Grammar | Best Output Depth |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| A1 | daily survival words | short direct sentences | be/do, present tense, simple questions | explain slowly, give simple replacements |
| A2 | life routines and common topics | short connected sentences | past/future, comparatives, basic modals | add scene examples and safe patterns |
| B1 | daily opinions and stories | connected paragraphs | conditionals, relative clauses, common phrasal verbs | explain tone and reusable chunks |
| B2 | natural opinions and nuance | longer spoken turns | hedging, emphasis, idioms, discourse markers | highlight register, subtext, and alternatives |
| C1 | abstract or dense content | complex arguments | advanced collocations, irony, layered tone | explain cultural nuance and rhetorical choices |
| C2 | near-native density | flexible and idiomatic | highly compressed meaning | focus on precision, style, and implication |

## Exam Tracks

- IELTS Speaking: give flexible answer frames, topic vocabulary, fluency chunks, and pronunciation focus.
- TOEFL: give academic paraphrase, summary structure, and integrated-speaking expressions.
- Business English: give polite disagreement, meeting language, email phrasing, risk language, and concise alternatives.

## Japanese JLPT

| Level | Vocabulary | Sentence Shape | Grammar | Best Output Depth |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| N5 | basic daily nouns, verbs, adjectives | short polite sentences | です/ます, particles, basic verb forms | add kana and very plain examples |
| N4 | daily routines and simple opinions | simple connected speech | て形, ない形, た形, 普通形 | explain particles and basic tone |
| N3 | everyday complex expression | natural casual/polite mix | passive, causative, conditionals, てしまう | explain nuance, scene, and natural alternatives |
| N2 | workplace/news/social topics | longer abstract sentences | honorific basics, written expressions, complex connectors | explain register and indirectness |
| N1 | dense written and spoken nuance | highly compressed or rhetorical | advanced idioms, formal style, subtle stance | explain implication, style, and social distance |

## Judgment Rules

- Judge level by the hardest repeated feature, not one isolated word.
- When a passage mixes levels, label the main learning level and mention stretch points.
- For beginners, reduce the number of key sentences and make shadowing shorter.
- For advanced learners, add nuance, register, collocations, and rewrite options.
