When I first started learning a new language, I thought progress meant memorizing more words every day. Later I realized that words only become useful when I can use them in a real situation. For example, it is one thing to know the phrase "I am not sure", but it is another thing to use it politely in a meeting when I disagree with an idea.

Now I study with short video clips. I listen once for the general meaning, then I choose three sentences that I might actually say in my own life. I write down the sentence, the situation, and one small change I can make. After that, I read it slowly, copy the rhythm, and try to say it without looking.

This method is not fast, but it feels real. I do not want to collect hundreds of expressions that I never use. I would rather learn five useful sentences deeply and make them part of my voice.
